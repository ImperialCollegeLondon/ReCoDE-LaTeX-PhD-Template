## 4. Images and Figures in Theses

- Use **high-resolution images** that are legible when printed.  
- Figures should have **consistent captions, numbering, and placement** in line with LaTeX template standards.  
- **Credit sources** appropriately for non-original material.  
- Do **not include images that infringe copyright** or proprietary restrictions.  
- Figures should **enhance the narrative**, demonstrating research results, methodology, or context.

---

## 5. Typography and Colour

- **Primary font for body text:** LaTeX default serif (Times/Latin Modern) or template default.  
- **Headings and figure labels:** Consistent and legible; maintain hierarchy.  
- **Colour palette:** Use Imperial-approved colours when adding emphasis or highlighting figures.  
- Avoid excessive decorative styling or unapproved colours that reduce clarity.

---

## 7. Style for Figures, Tables, and Supplementary Graphics

- **Captions:** Short, informative, consistent formatting.  
- **Tables:** Align text clearly, avoid overcrowding.  
- **Supplementary graphics:** Place in appendices if not essential to examination.
