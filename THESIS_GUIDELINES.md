## 1. General Requirements

Every PhD candidate is required to submit a **thesis** that represents the culmination of their research work undertaken during the period of registration under supervision for the degree.

The thesis must demonstrate:
- **Originality and independent critical thinking**  
- **Coherence and integration** of argument and results  
- **Adherence** to the academic and presentation standards prescribed by the College  

---

## 2. Composition of the Thesis

The thesis shall:

### (a) Authorship and Supervision
- Consist of the **candidateâ€™s own account** of their investigations, the greater proportion of which must have been conducted **under supervision during registration** for the degree.  
- If the research involved **collaborative work**, the **candidateâ€™s specific contribution** must be **clearly identified** within the thesis and **certified by the supervisor**.

### (b) Original Contribution to Knowledge
- Form a **distinct and original contribution** to the knowledge of the subject.  
- Provide clear **evidence of originality** through:
  - The discovery of new facts, and/or  
  - The exercise of **independent critical power**.  

---

### (c) Coherence and Integration
- The thesis must be an **integrated whole**, presenting a **coherent argument**.

### (d) Structure, Literature Review, and Research Discussion
The thesis should:
- Provide a **critical assessment** of the relevant literature.  
- Describe the **research methods** and **findings** in detail.  
- Include a **discussion** of those findings in the context of the field.  
- Demonstrate:
  - A **deep and synoptic understanding** of the discipline.  
  - The ability to place the work in a **broader academic and practical context**.  
  - **Objectivity, judgment, and autonomy** in approaching complex research problems.  

---

### (e) Language and Presentation
- The thesis must be **written in English**.  
- The **literary presentation** (clarity, grammar, and structure) must be **of a satisfactory academic standard**.  

---

### (f) Word Limit
- The total length of the thesis **must not exceed 100,000 words**.

## 3. Recommended Best Practices

To meet and exceed College expectations:
- Begin writing early and revise frequently to maintain coherence.  
- Maintain clear records of individual contributions in collaborative research.  
- Consult with your **supervisor** regularly on structure, content, and compliance.  
- Use a **version control system** (e.g., Git) or collaborative platform like **Overleaf** to manage drafts and backups.  
- Verify your formatting and structure against the **Imperial Thesis LaTeX Template** before submission.
